## THE 6 DEMOGRAPHICS

### 1. KIDS
- **What they want:** Coolness, fun, making friends, expressing themselves
- **Visual cues:** Playful, energetic, YOUNG faces in movement
- **Messaging:** "Have fun, make friends, express yourself"
- **Tone:** Approachable, playful (but NOT cartoonish)

### 2. TEENS
- **What they want:** K-pop, cool styles, peer community, creativity, self-expression
- **Visual cues:** Modern, aspirational, K-pop/contemporary dancers
- **Messaging:** "Cool moves, creative energy, peer community"
- **Tone:** Confident, modern, aspirational

### 3. YOUNG ADULTS
- **What they want:** Fitness, social connection, learning new skills, fun
- **Visual cues:** Energetic, social, fit, contemporary/hip-hop styles
- **Messaging:** "Get fit, make friends, learn cool moves"
- **Tone:** Energetic, inclusive, growth-oriented

### 4. ADULTS
- **What they want:** Salsa/Bachata, fitness, stress relief, beginner-friendly
- **Visual cues:** Salsa/Bachata couples, adult learners smiling, joy
- **Messaging:** "Never too late, stress relief, social, beginner-friendly"
- **Tone:** Welcoming, empowering, supportive

### 5. ACTIVE ADULTS (50+)
- **What they want:** Health, vitality, gentle movement, friendship
- **Visual cues:** Older adults moving, smiling, in community, joyful
- **Messaging:** "Health, vitality, friendship, gentle movement"
- **Tone:** Respectful, energetic, inclusive

### 6. STUDIO RENTALS
- **What they want:** Professional facilities, flexibility, variety of uses
- **Visual cues:** Professional studio space, events, corporate groups, clean setup
- **Messaging:** "Flexible rental, professional facilities, community events"
- **Tone:** Professional, practical, accessible
- **Note:** This is ~50% of revenue â€” CANNOT be secondary

## RESEARCH: MULTI-DEMOGRAPHIC WEBSITES

### Key Insight from Research
Real websites that serve multiple demographics do these things:

**1. Clear Navigation by Demographic (Classes filtered by age)**
- Born 2 Dance: Has separate /adults, /teens, /kids landing pages
- CityDance: Explicit "Locations" + "Class Types" + age ranges listed
- American Dance Institute: "Classes by age group: 2.5-3.5 | 3.5-7 | 8-11 | 11-15 | 15+"
- Fred Astaire: "Students of every age and ability"

**2. No Single Hero Image (Avoids Alienation)**
- Multi-generational community centers show diverse people AT DIFFERENT AGES
- They don't lead with one demographic
- They show "we serve everyone" visually upfront

**3. Explicit "This is for you" Signposting**
- Tempe City Community Center: Filter by keyword, location, category, timing, **age group**
- Brampton Recreation: Step-by-step guides to help non-tech-savvy users
- Honolulu Dance: "We accept students aged 3 years to adult, basic to professional levels"

**4. Genre/Style Diversity in Imagery**
- Born 2 Dance: Hip Hop (young), Salsa (adults), Contemporary (teens/young adults)
- Multiple dance styles visible = multiple age groups visible

**5. Rental Spaces Given EQUAL WEIGHT**
- Community centers show: youth programs, adult programs, fitness, AND event rental all equally
- Not: "classes are main, rentals are secondary"

## PATTERNS: HOW TO AVOID ALIENATION

### Pattern 1: Identity Grid at Top
âœ… Shows immediately: "All these demographics exist here"  
âœ… Visual proof that someone like you is welcome  
âŒ Without it: 70-year-old sees K-pop dancer â†’ bounces  

**Examples found:** Most sites don't do this explicitly, BUT the ones that succeed have:
- Clear navigation labels (Kids | Teens | Adults | Seniors | Rentals)
- Visible from top of page (not buried in menu)

### Pattern 2: Demographic Vignettes While Scrolling
âœ… Each demographic gets its own "section of proof"  
âœ… Shows someone like you in the space  
âŒ Without it: Feels like you're an afterthought

**How Born 2 Dance does it:**
- Shows: Hip Hop for kids (energetic, fun)
- Shows: Salsa for adults (sensual, partnered)
- Shows: K-POP for teens (aspirational, cool)
- = Each person scrolling sees "oh, there's MY thing"

### Pattern 3: Diverse Imagery (Multiple Styles)
âœ… Hip Hop (appeals to younger), Salsa (appeals to adults), Ballet (appeals to kids/adults)  
âœ… Each style has different-aged people  
âŒ If only K-pop shown: alienates older demographics

### Pattern 4: Explicit Beginner Messaging
âœ… "Everyone here started exactly where you are"  
âœ… "Never too late to learn"  
âœ… "Good teaching removes fear"  
âŒ "Elite training" or "advanced only" = alienates beginners

### Pattern 5: Rentals Given Visual Weight
âœ… Rental section shown as equal to classes (not buried)  
âœ… "Professional facilities, flexible rental" messaging  
âŒ If rentals seem secondary: loses 50% of potential revenue message

## DESIGN PRINCIPLES (NO MARKETING JARGON)

### DO âœ…
- Warm, grounded, authentic language

- Beginner-friendly throughout
- Community, belonging, friendship as themes
- Multiple dance styles (= multiple age appeal)
- Clear visual hierarchy (identity grid â†’ demographic sections)
